Disarmament and International Security Committee

Committee Description

Topic A: Demobilization of Nuclear Weapons

In the shadowy depths of international politics, a dangerous game of power and brinkmanship unfolds, threatening to destabilize regions and redefine global dynamics. Today, we find ourselves at the crossroads of multiple flashpoints that reverberate with the echoes of past conflicts and fuel the anxieties of a world on edge. From the deployment of nuclear weapons by Russia within Belarus to the escalating tensions in the Korean Peninsula, we see that a discuss of demobilizing nuclear weapons must be on the table. As delegates of the Disarmament and International Security Committee (DISEC) , it is your job to face the complex challenges of disarmament of nuclear bombs, and avoid a nuclear disaster.

Topic B: Disarming Biological and Chemical Weapons

These weapons, born from the perverted fusion of science and destruction, possess the capacity to unleash unimaginable suffering upon humanity. From genetically engineered pathogens to lethal chemical agents, they infiltrate silently, leaving devastation in their wake. The ease of proliferation in our interconnected world demands unwavering attention, calling for strengthened international cooperation and stringent enforcement of existing conventions. As DISEC, it is your job to deliver in your duty to making the world safer, and that includes reducing the use of biologicla and chemical weapons.
